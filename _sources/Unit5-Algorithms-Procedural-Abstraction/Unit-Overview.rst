    <p class="overview">
      This unit examines algorithms and procedures in more detail. The Logo apps, started in Unit 4 and continued in Unit 5, introduce the concept of <b>procedural abstraction </b>and you will learn to define and use procedures -- named blocks of code that perform a specific task -- as well as when and how to use <b>procedures with parameters</b>. By encapsulating the algorithms into named procedures and introducing <span class="hover vocab yui-wk-div" data-id='parameters'>parameters</span> to help generalize the algorithms, you will be able to see the advantages of procedural abstraction. In addition to designing and testing your own algorithms,Â  you will be introduced to the <b>analysis of algorithms</b>. You will compare various <b>searching</b> and <b>sorting</b> algorithms, including examining the <span class="hover vocab yui-wk-div" data-id='efficiency'>efficiency</span> of each. For the searching and sorting algorithms, you will analyze them both experimentally and through mathematical concepts such as functions and graph. The impact section of this unit provides another opportunity for you to explore a computing innovation and the effects it has on society.</p>
